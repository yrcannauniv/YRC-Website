(Blood Donation Camp)
(For more details view the site in Desktop)


"Donate The Blood For The Reason And The Reason Is To Save Life"

Date : 12.03.2025 (Thursday)

Venue : CEG – Vivekananda Auditorium, Anna University

Beneficiary : Indian Red Cross Society Blood Bank, Chennai-8

No. of Units : 157 Units

he blood donation camp was organized by the Youth Red Cross Volunteers (YRC) of College of Engineering Guindy - in collaboration with the Indian Red Cross Society (IRCS) and sponsored by HDFC Bank. The primary objective of blood donation was to collect blood from donors of various blood groups. These collected units will be utilized by the Indian Red Cross Society to support patients in dire need of blood transfusions, including accident victims, patients undergoing surgery, individuals with chronic illnesses, and those suffering from blood disorders.

he event took place at Vivekananda Auditorium at 9:30 AM, The camp was inaugurated by Dr. K. S. Eswara Kumar, Dean of CEG, along with esteemed dignitaries including Dr. S. Thanigaiararu, YRC University Coordinator, Mr. N. Deva Pichai, Corporate Social Responsibility Manager, and Mr. Syed, Head Organizer - IRCS. .It was a great success with more than 251 registrations and 157 donations. The camp started at 9.30 am went on till 3.30 pm. An experienced medical team from the blood bank of Indian Red Cross Society collected blood from the donors.

Sukaraksha'17

(Blood Donation Camp)
(For more details view the site in Desktop)



"Blood is meant to circulate, so pass it around!"
Date : 07.09.2017 (Friday)

Venue : CEG - Alumni Centre, Anna University

Beneficiary : Blood Bank of Royapettah Government Hospital

No. of Units : 134 Units

The Doctors with Medical Teams from the Royapettah Government Hospital arrived at the Camp site around 9:00 a.m. The camp inaugurated by Mr.D.Narasimhan, YRC Programme Officer donating the blood.


Then, the students started pouring in. Without any second thought, sans expectations to get anything in return but with the thought to save a life, many donated their blood. The students registered their name, department, blood type and phone numbers in the Registration Form. Their weights were noted and based on their hemoglobin count they were checked if they were eligible to donate blood. The donors were handed refreshments after they donated blood and presented with a certificate for their priceless service to humanity. Not just students, many teaching and non teaching staffs of Anna university also graced the event with their presence and contributed their blood.

The event happened with such vigour and excitement. More than 200 registrations and 134 donors, the camp was an eminent success and came to close around 3:00 p.m. after which the blood was safely taken by the hospital authorities back to the blood bank for use under emergencies. This camp stands as a testimony to every student's social concern and moral instinct to serve the society.

Sukaraksha'16

(Blood Donation Camp)


Date : 31.08.2016 (Wednesday)

Venue : CEG - Alumni Centre, Anna University

Beneficiary : Blood Bank of Cancer Institute, Chennai-20

No. of Units : 164 units

The Youth Red Cross of CEG Campus, Anna University jointly with Cancer Institute, Adyar organised a Blood Donation Camp named SUKARAKSHA'16 on 31.08.2016 (Wednesday) at the CEG-Alumni Centre, Anna University. A Medical Team from Cancer Institute Blood Bank arrived at 9:00 am with all the necessary equipments and the camp was inaugurated by Mr.D.Narashiman, YRC Programme Officer, CEG Campus around 9:30 am. The donors were first made to register.

Then they underwent a test to know their Blood Group and Hemoglobin content followed by BP check done by an experienced medical officer. Based on the test results, the donors who were considered eligible to donated about 350 ml of blood. Finally refreshments were provided and a Certificate of Appreciation was given by Cancer Institute. Students, Ph.D. Scholars, Teaching and Non-Teaching Staffs of Anna University donated blood. Dr.P.Narayanasamy, Dean, CEG Campus, Dr.B.Kumar, YRC Programme Coordinator and all the other units YRC Programme Officers of CEG Campus supervised the camp and gave moral support.About 164 units of blood had been collected. The camp came to an end by 3.30 pm.
